3.06 Oxidative Pathways

Glucose catabolism begins with glycolosis

  • Cells must break down glucose to extract energy for ATP synthesis
  • Glycolysis: breaking glucose
    • 10-step pathway, 10 enzymes, results in 2 molecules of pyruvate
    • All enzymes are located in cytoplasm of both prokaryotic and eukaryotic cells
    • Net input: 1 molecule glucose, 2 NAD+, 2 ADP
    • Net output: 2 molecules pyruvate, 2 NADH, 2 ATP
      • 2 ATP hydrolyzed in initial stages, but 4 ATP synthesized in later stages, so net production of 2 ATP
    • Only extracts small fraction of potential energy from glucose

Pyruvate is oxidized to acetyl-CoA and CO2

  • In respiring cells, 2 molecules of pyruvate are each:
    • decarboxylated (one of the 3 carbons comes off as CO2)
    • oxidized (electrons transferred to NAD+ to make NADH)
    • covalently bonded to Coenzyme A (CoA) to make acetyl-CoA
  • All steps done by single enzyme complex: pyruvate dehydrogenase
    • Input (per glucose molecule): 2 pyruvate, 2 CoA, 2 NAD+
    • Output (per glucose molecule): 2 acetyl-CoA, 2 CO2, 2 NADH
  • Pyruvate oxidation occurs in cytoplasm of prokaryotes, mitochondrial matrix of eukaryotic cells

Acetyl-CoA enters the Citric Acid Cycle

  • Combines with 4-carbon oxaloacetate to form 6-carbon citrate + free CoA
  • Citric Acid Cycle releases 2 carbons from citric acid as carbon dioxide through redox reactions
    • Reduce electron carriers to produce 3 NADH, 1 FADH2, 1 ATP through substrate-level phosphorylation, along with oxaloacetate, ready to accept another acetyl-CoA
  • Inputs (per glucose molecule): 2 Acetyl-CoA, 6 NAD+, 2 FAD, 2 ADP
  • Outputs (per glucose molecule): 2 CoA, 4 CO2, 6 NADH, 2 FADH2, 2 ATP
  • Occurs in cytoplasm of prokaryotic cells, mitochondrial matrix of eukaryotic cells

Net ATP production per molecule of glucose oxidized

  • Max ATP production is during aerobic respiration, reduced electron carriers dump electrons to transport chain to power ATP synthesis
  • Prokaryotes: total ATP production from ox. phosphorylation & substrate-level phosphorylation can be as high as 38 ATP per glucose molecule
  • Eukaryotes: slightly lower because of cost of transporting pyruvate into mitochondria
    • commonly 36 ATP per glucose molecule

Protein Metabolism

  • Proteins first hydrolyzed to individual amino acids
  • Amino group nitrogens stripped off, sent to urea cycle, remaining carbon converted to pyruvate, acetyl-CoA, or other intermediates depending on amino acid

Fat Metabolism

  • Fats hydrolyzed to glycerol & fatty acids
  • Glycerol converted to glycolysis intermidiate upstream of pyruvate, fatty acids oxidized to acetyl-CoA 2 carbons at a time
  • Beta-oxidation (metabolism of fatty acids) happens in mitochondrial matrix, generates reduced electron carriers & acetyl-CoA to be further oxidized in citric acid cycle
