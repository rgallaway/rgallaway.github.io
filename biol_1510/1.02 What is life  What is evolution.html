1.02 What is life? What is evolution?

Evolution as an emergent property of life

  • Reproduction is imperfect, creating variation over generations
  • Population grows exponentially, but reaches limit where individuals compete for resources
    • Individuals with superior variants will reproduce more
  • Biological Evolution: change in heritable characteristics of a population over succeeding generations
    • Change in gene pool of a population
  • Not just natural selection: random processes can contribute, especially in small populations

Evolution is a theory, not just a hypothesis

  • Darwinâ€™s theories have been tested multiple times, results always consistent
  • Evolutionary theory is a framework of biological thinking
  • Supporting evidence:
    • Geological & fossil record shows Earth is ~4.5b years old, sequential changes in organisms
    • Homologies in body plans, structures, DNA indicate common ancestry
      • Homology: shared ancestry between structures/genes in different species
    • Common biochemistry: amino acids, genetic code, etc
    • Gene sequence comparisons agree w/ fossil record

Three domains of life

  • Bacteria, Archaea, Eukaryota
    • Bacteria & Archaea are prokaryotes: single-celled with no nucleus
    • Eukaryota includes everything else with nuclei in cells, arose a billion years after prokaryotes
    • People have all 3 in their bodies
