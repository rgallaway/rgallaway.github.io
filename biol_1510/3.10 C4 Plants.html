3.10 C4 Plants

Rubisco’s Oxygenase Activity Impairs Photosynthetic Efficiency

  • Sometimes fixes O2 to RuBP instead of CO2
    • Occurs in low CO2, high O2 conditions, pronounced at high temperature
    • Produces 2-phosphoglycolate: toxic compound, reacts to release CO2 and results in loss of organic carbon and energy production
      • Called photorespiration
  • Evolved before oxygenic photosynthesis, so no selection against oxygenate activity
    • Nothing has eliminated it without also affecting carboxylase activity

C4 Plants Have Evolved a Mechanism to Deliver CO2 to Rubisco

  • Some plants’ first product of carbon fixation is 4-carbon compound, hence “C4”
  • Use alternate enzyme, PEP carboxylase, which:
    • has no oxygenase activity
    • has higher affinity for CO2 than Rubisco
  • Specialized leaf anatomy: two differenty types of photosynthetic cells (mesophyll and bundle sheath)
    • Mesophyll cells use PEP carboxylase to fix CO2 and synthesize malate (4-carbon)
    • Transported to bundle sheath cells clustered around vascular bundles (xylem & phloem)
    • Decarboxylated to release pyruvate & CO2 -> fixed by Rubisco as for C3 plants
    • Pyruvate returns to mesophyll, phosphate from ATP used to regenerate PEP
  • C4 fixation has added cost of 1 ATP for every CO2

Advantages and Disadvantages of C4 and C3 Carbon Fixation

  • C4 grow better under hot, dry conditions
    • With stomata closed, CO2 levels fall, O2 levels rise
  • C3 grow better under cool, moist conditions with stomata open
    • C3 plants don’t incur additional ATP cost of C4 fixation

Photosynthesis and Respiration: Mirror Images?

  • Chemical equations for photosynthesis & aerobic respiration are exactly reverse of each other
  • Balance between photosynthesis & respiration global rates is needed to maintain stable atmospheric concentrations of CO2 and O2
  • In eukaryotes, both photosynthesis & respiration are in organelles with double membranes & circular genomes
    • Originated as prokaryotic endosymbionts
  • Both have ETCs, chemiosmosis, ATP synthase powered by proton motive force
