4.04 Patterns of Inheritance

Beyond Dominant/Recessive Traits

  • Mendelâ€™s rules are the simplest pattern possible, but most traits are NOT controlled this way
  • Other, more common patterns:
    • Incomplete dominance - heterozygotes have intermediate phenotype in between homozygous phenotypes
      • Example: homozygous flowers are white or red, heterozygotes are pink
    • Co-dominance - heterozygotes display each phenotype associated with each allele
      • Example: blood type in humans - only A alleles is type A, B alleles are type B, both makes type AB
    • Quantitative traits - continuous phenotype controlled by additive alleles at multiple genes
      • Trait controlled by multiple genes (polygenic inheritance) each having multiple alleles
      • Example: human height
      • In contrast to discrete traits with just a few phenotypes
    • Multiple allelism - particular gene has more than two alleles
      • Example: blood type in humans has A, B, or O allele
    • Gene by environment interactions - environment plays a role in determining phenotype controlled by alleles
      • Example: human height: childhood nutrition plays a role in adult height
  • All still controlled by chromosome behavior during meiosis
  • Most traits are controlled by one or more of these patterns above
