3.01 Biological Molecules

All living organisms are made of organic molecules

  • Organic molecules must have C & H, may have O, N, P, S
  • Have at least one bond between C and H or C and C (carbon atom in a reduced state, rather than fully oxidized)
    • Exception is urea, where carbon bonds to 2 amino nitrogens and double bond with oxygen
  • Can arise from abiotic synthesis (Miller-Urey), but most are synthesized by living organisms
    • Requires energy and chemical reducing power (redox reaction)

The biomass of a cell is composed of 3 types of macromolecules plus lipids

  • Polysaccharides, nucleic acids (DNA & RNA), proteins

Small organic molecules are assembled to form 3 tpes of large polymers; lipid membranes self-assemble

  • Cells are composed of 68 distinct organic molecules, assembled into 4 macromolecules: 3 polymers plus lipid membranes
  • Polymerization occurs by dehydration reactions - linking 2 subunits via covalent bond extracts -OH and H to create water molecule
  • Cleavage of polymers occurs by hydrolysis reactions - molecule of water is split to break the bond linking subunits
  • Lipids spontaneously aggregate in hydrophobic interactions to form membranes
  • Descriptions:
    • Glycans (polysaccharides) - carbohydrates may refer to monomers or polysaccharides made by linking monosaccharides via glycosidic bonds. Complex carbs often have branched structures
    • Nucleic acids (RNA & DNA) - polymers made by joining nucleotides in a phosphodiester linkage. 
      • RNA uses ribose, has uracil base, DNA uses deoxyribose with thymine base. 
      • RNA single-stranded, DNA double-stranded.
      • Hydrogen bonding between A:T and G:C stabilize RNA duplexes and RNA secondary structures
    • Polypeptides (proteins) - polymers of amino acids joined by peptide bonds
      • Secondary, tertiary, sometimes quaternary structures stabilized by various bonds and interactions
    • Lipid bilayer membranes - lipids create boundaries & hydrophobic environment separating interior from exterior of cell, separates distinct organelle compartments.
      • Essential to maintain thermodynamic disequilibrium for energy metabolism

Cells use different classes of biological macromolecules in different ways

  • Polysaccharides used primarily for energy storage, static structures, but play important roles in cell-cell recognition, adhesion, signaling
  • Proteins used primarily for enyzmatic activies, signaling, dynamic structural components
  • Nucleic acids used for genetic information storage & retrieval. Some RNA play catalytic roles in information processing
  • Lipids used to define cell boundary, compartmentalize, energy storage, and signaling

Cells and 2 types of nucleic acids: DNA & RNA, that differ in key ways

  • DNA has bases A, C, G, and T, deoxyribose, 2 strands that form duplex with hydrogen bonds between bases
    • Function is storage & transmission of hereditary information
  • RNA has bases A, C, G, and U, ribose, one strand that may form internal duplexes by folding on itself
    • Functions in expression of genetic info in DNA to make proteins, may also serve for storage of hereditary information in many viruses
