1.09 Mass Extinctions and Radiations

End Permian Extinction (251-252 mya)

  • Greatest mass extinction in Phanerozoic, 95% of species perished
  • Photosynthesis declined, atmospheric CO2 increased by 2000 ppm, temperature increased 10 C
  • Potential causes:
    • Vulcanism - Siberian Traps eruption occurred, covered 2 million km2 with lava
    • Greenhouse gases & global warming - CO2 releases from volcanos warmed oceans
    • Changes in ocean chemistry - Positive feedback loop of warming oceans -> more greenhouse gases amplifies global warming, results in anoxic oceans
    • Release of toxic gases - One hypothesis about sulfate-releasing bacteria releasing hydrogen sulfide gas

End-Cretaceous Extinction

  • Latest mass extinction, associated with extinction of the dinosaurs
  • Coincides with meteor impact that created Chicxulub crater
    • Ejected dust into atmosphere, blocking sunlight, cooling planet, reducing photosynthesis
  • Much evidence for meteor causing extinction, but other things may have predated impact contributing to extinction

The Cambrian Explosion and Other Major Evolutionary Radiations

  • Life recovered after each extinction, attained higher biodiversity than before
  • Cambrian Explosion refers to increase in biodiversity
    • First multicellular organisms replaced by Cambrian fauna, includes all body plans of present-day phyla
    • Spans several million years, did not all appear simultaneously
      • Coincides with increase of free oxygen to near present-day levels
  • Periods of rapid speciation & increasing biodiversity are called radiations
    • Occur when new niches are available, such as after a mass extinction
    • Also occur in response to evolutionary innovation, such as the evolution of jaws
  • Increase in oxygen enabled evolution of larger bodies, organs, tissues, such as brains
    • Evolution of life changed the planet by filling it with oxygen
