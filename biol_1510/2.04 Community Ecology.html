2.04 Community Ecology

Resources and Niche Concept

  • Resource - causes population growth and is reduced by use
    • Resource reduced most by use (limits population growth first) is called limiting resource
  • Niche - full range of resources a species can use, combined with range of conditions it can tolerate
    • Complex niches usually reduced to dimensions most important for question
    • Fundamental niche is the ful range, realized niche is the part actually used in nature
    • Competition between species is strongest where niches overlap

Competitive iterations have negative effects on both species

Interspecific Competition - competition for resources that drives reductions in niche overlap (negative effect)
  • Individuals would have more success in absence of other species
  • Slight alteration of logistic equation
$$\frac{dN_1}{dt}=r_1N_1\left(\frac{K_1-N_1-\alpha_{12}N_2}{K_1}\right)$$ $\cdot$ $\alpha$ is the competition coefficient - converting units of species 2 into units of species 1 For more individuals of species 2 or larger $\alpha$, species 1 sees lower population growth.

Predation, Parasitism, and Herbivory are detrimental to one species while beneficial to the other

  • Predation: one species consuming the other
    • Win-lose scenario, sets up for food web dynamics
  • Parasitism: one species (parasite) lives on or in another species (host) and harms it over time
    • Win for parasite, loss for host
  • Herbivory: Similar to predation, but could be considered beneficial because it can promote growth of new plants
    • Must measure cost & benefit to the plant

Mutualisms benefit both players in the interspecific interaction

  • Both species benefit from interaction, but vulnerable to cheating

Interspecific interactions are not static but can evolve

  • Mutualism cheating can shift into parasitism
  • Parasitism can evolve into mutualism
