1.03 Evolution by Natural Selection

Evolution by Natural Selection

  • Darwinâ€™s 4 requirements:
    • Trait must be variable: encoding gene has more than one variant (allele)
    • Trait must be heritable
    • Struggle of existence: more offspring born than environment can support
    • Differential survival and reproduction governed by fit of organism to environment
  • Fitness: organisms that best match envornment have greater survival & reproduction than lesser matches
    • Resulting heritable trait is called an adaptation

Common mistakes in thinking about Evolution by Natural Selection

  • Individuals do not evolve; instead, population is evolving
  • No fixed endpoint or best phenotype. Environment serves as selective agent
  • Organisms do not behave for the good of the species; each individual lives and reproduces
  • Not always best possible fit because of constraints and trade-offs

Why evolution matters to ordinary people

  • Bacteria evolving resistance to antibiotics
  • Cancer: individual cells acquire mutations that are passed on in mitosis

Phylogenetic trees

  • Diagram of evolutionary relationships among organisms
  • Branches (vertical lines) represent lineage
  • Nodes are where they diverge (speciation event from common ancestor)
  • Trunk at base is the root, represents most recent common ancestor of all the taxons on the tree
  • Clade: group of taxons including common ancestor and all descendants
    • Clades are monophyletic
