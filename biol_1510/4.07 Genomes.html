4.07 Genomes

Prokaryotic Genomes

  • Genomes of Bacteria & Archaea compact; essentially all DNA is functional
  • Sizes of genomes range from 1 million to 10 million base pairs, usually in single circular chromosome
  • Genes in biochemical pathway or signaling pathway are often clustered, arranged in operons, where they are transcribed as single mRNA that is translated to make all proteins in operon
  • Size of genomes directly related to metabolic capabilities - more genes, more proteins & enzymes

Eukaryotic Genomes

  • Genome sizes highly variable, even within taxonomic groups (C-value paradox)
  • Divided into multiple linear chromosomes, each containing single linear duplex DNA molecule
  • Eukaryotic genes in biochemical or signaling pathway are not organized into operons; one mRNA makes one protein
  • Multiple exons can be spliced in different ways to make multiple mRNAs & proteins from a single gene (Alternative Splicing)
  • Majority of human genes can be spliced in 2+ different ways, so actual number of proteins exceeds number of protein-coding genes

What accounts for variation in genome size?

  • No correlation betwenn body size or complexity and size of genome
    • So far, range from 6,000 to 30,000 protein-coding genes (less than 10-fold variation)
    • High variation due to varying amounts of non-coding DNA

  • Protein-coding (exon) sequences comprise less than 2%
  • Introns just over 1/4
  • Transposable elements & derivative DNA make up 1/2
    • Transposons, LTR retrotransposons, LINEs, SINEs
  • SINE family Alu element is a 300-nucleotide sequence present in over 1 million copies in human & chimp genomes
  • Segmental duplications are long segments that have become duplicated
    • Create copies of genes that can mutate & acquire new functions

Human genome: 80% junk or 80% functional?

  • ENCODE project claims that 80% has at least some biochemical activity
  • microRNAs (miRNAs) are transcribed from intergenic regions
    • Comprise less than 1% of genome
    • Other studies show only 10% subject to evolutionary constraint

DNA sequencing

  • Human genome project accomplished by automated sequences that used Sanger dideoxy sequencing
  • Parallel sequencing technologies have brought down cost & throughput of DNA sequencing
