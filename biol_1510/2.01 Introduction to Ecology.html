2.01 Introduction to Ecology

Ecology and its Sub-Disciplines

  • Study of how organisms interact with environment
  • Behavior: how individual responds to stimulus
  • Population ecology: how indivs of same species interact with each other
  • Ecosystem ecology: how organisms interact with non-living things in the environment
  • Biosphere: entire set of interactions

Climate Patterns affect where communities live in the biosphere

  • Orientation of earthâ€™s axis toward sun, heat retention in atmosphere, rotation of the earth
  • Atmosphere-ocean system is a large heat engine
    • Sunlight at equator heats water & air, water becomes vapor and rises into the atmosphere
    • Rising air cools, causing precipitation, warm dry air is pushed out of the way by expanding hot air
    • Cool air falls back to earth without moisture
    • High pressure redistributes to low pressure areas, such as equator, creating air conveyor
  • Scaled up, Hadley cell & companions establish north-south gradient
    • Disrupted by Coriolis effect, establishing prevailing wind patterns
  • Communities are classified by temperature & precipitation profiles into biomes (terrestrial, aquatic, or marine)
  • Map of biomes in the world shows global patterns, such as desert & forests organized by latitude
    • Interruptions occur when features run counter to latitude
      • Andes in S. America north-to-south, disrupting east-west patterns in Africa
  • By categorizing biomes by temperature & precipitation, we can graphically organize to predict effect of environmental changes
  • In water biomes, precipitation matters less, temperature & winds take more dominant role
    • Ocean upwelling: Wind pushes offshore, causing water from ocean bottom to rise to surface, bringing nutrients up with it
    • In freshwater systems, seasonal temperature change causes turnover
      • Stratified temperature layers in winter, nutrients settle to bottom
      • Warming in the spring causes suface water to sink, pushing deeper water to surface and turning over nutrients, same in the fall

Subtropical desert: 30 degrees N & S, extreme temp & aridity

Tropical wet forest: equatorial, favorable growing conditions, high productivity & diversity

Temperate grassland: Fluctuating temperatures, not great for growth

Arctic tundra: Low diversity & productivity, permafrost, surface covered in plants

Boreal forests: Low productivity but high biomass, low diversity

Temperate forests: Moderate diversity, fluctuating temperatures, moderate precipitation
