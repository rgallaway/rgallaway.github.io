3.09 Carbon Fixation

Where do Plants Get Their Biomass?

  • Biomass of trees (largely carbohydrates) comes from Carbon Dioxide
    • Only hydrogen atoms from water molecules contribute

How Calvin, Benson, and Bassham worked out carbon fixation

  • Used radioactive 14CO2 to determine first products of carbon fixation
    • Within 10 minutes after injection, many organic molecules were labeled
    • At shorter times, discovered first product was a 3-carbon sugar: 3-phosphoglycerate
  • ATP and NADPH used to reduce 3-PG to glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate (G3P)
    • 2 G3P can spontaneously form glucose molecule
  • Deduced it was a cyclic process
    • Predicted that in the dark, 3PG accumulates becuase ATP and NADPH canâ€™t be made
    • In absence of CO2, CO2 acceptor would increase
  • Acceptor turned out to be 5-carbon sugar phosphate: ribulose-1,5-biphosphate (RuBP)
    • Rubisco enzyme attaches CO2 to RuBP to form 6-carbon intermediate that breaks down to 2 3PG
  • Steps:
    • Every 3 molecules CO2 fixed by Rubisco produces 6 3PG
    • Conversion of 6 3PG to 6 G3P consumes 6 ATP and 6 NADPH
    • One G3P exits cycle, leaving 5 G3P
    • 5 G3P rearrange to from 3 ribulose-5-phosphate
    • Phosphorylation of 3 ribulose-5-phosphate to restore 3 ribulose-1,5-biphosphate consumes 3 ATP
  • Products of Calvin cycle are exported from chloroplast to power respiration, organic carbon for biosynthetic pathways
