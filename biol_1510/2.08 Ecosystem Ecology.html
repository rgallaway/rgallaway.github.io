2.08 Ecosystem Ecology

  • Populations labeled by how they fit into trophic interactions (feeding interactions) between species
  • Primary producers - capture sunlight and build into chemical bonds through photosynthesis
    • Primary consumers eat primary producers
    • Secondary consumers eat primary consumers
  • Detritivores eat dead organic matter
  • Interactions can be mapped into a food web connecting victim to enemy

Energy flows but matter cycles

  • Not all energy is transferred on consumption, on average 90% is lost at each level
    • Pop size of top predators remains small, size & biomass of producers is huge
    • Ecology biomass refers to entire organism
  • All matter is either incorporated into consumer or left behind in environment
  • Toxins are incorporated into consumer, accumulating higher concentrations with each trophic position
    • Biomagnification
  • All primary production can be quantified as gross primary production
    • Remaining energy after metabolism is net primary production
    • Most NPP occurs in oceans, but greatest NPP/area is tropic rainforest

Nutrient Cycles

  • Matter is retained in some form in the ecosystem, stored in “compartments”
    • Flux because of respiration, photosynthesis, decomposition, burning
  • Carbon moves when organisms eat or die
    • Cycles very slowly in environment, much stored in tropical forests
  • Residence time of carbon in atmosphere is 3-4 years, more added than removed
