3.08 Photosynthesis

$$6 CO_2 + 12 H_2O \rightarrow C_6H_{12}O_6 + 6 O_2 + 6 H_2O$$
(Oxygen atoms in water are source of oxygen gas atoms)

Light Reaction Provides Both ATP and Reducing Power (NADH or NADPH) Needed for Carbon Fixation by the Calvin Cycle

Light as Energy

  • Visible light ranges from 400nm to around 700nm in wavelength
  • Wave-particle duality, photon is a quantum of light energy
  • Energy of a photon is inversely proportional to wavelength:
    • h is Planck’s constant, c is speed of light, λ is wavelength
$E = \frac{hc}{\lambda}$
  • Photons of shorter wavelengths have more energy than longer wavelengths

Biological Pigments

  • Molecules that absorb light at particular wavelengths
  • Organisms that use light energy for conversion to chemical energy have evolutionary & phylogenetic differences in pigments

Phototrophism vs Photosynthesis

  • Phototrophs convert light energy to chemical energy as ATP through photophosphorylation
  • Photosynthetic organisms (photoautotrophs) use light energy to make both ATP and reduced electron carriers (NADH, NADPH)
    • Reduction of CO2 to CH2O (carbohydrate) requires ATP and NADPH/NADH
      • NADP+ is used by chloroplasts instead of NAD+
  • Earliest phototrophs & photoautotrophs were prokaryotes, did not generate oxygen
    • 2 different photosystems evolved, combined in cyanobacteria
    • One system evolved power to oxidize water molecules as source of electrons, releasing O2

Phototrophic Archaea

  • No Archaea are truly photosynthetic
  • Halobacterium (actually Archaea) uses bacteriorhodopsin as light-driven proton pump to generate proton gradient for chemiosmosis
    • Doesn’t actually use light energy to fix CO2 to carbon, still depend on food molecules

Photosynthetic Bacteria

  • Photosynthetic bacteria and chloroplasts use variants of chlorophyll
    • Absorb blue & red light

Two Types of Photosystems

  • Photosystem: membrane complex of proteins & chlorophyll molecules
    • Chlorophyll absorbs protons & funnels energy to reaction center chlorophyll, which becomes oxidized
  • All oxygenic photosynthesizers have 2 types of photosystems coupled together
  • All anoxygenic photosynthetic bacteria have just one type
    • Earliest phototrophs probably anoxygenic
  • Photosystem I
    • Transfers electrons to ferredoxin protein, which reduces NADP+ to NADPH
      • Phosphorylated form of NAD+, P is phosphorus, also stands for Photosynthesis
  • Photosystem II
    • Transfers electrons to membrane-local electron transport chain
      • Pumps protons to generate proton gradient for chemiosmotic ATP synthesis
    • Oxidizes (splits) water molecules to regain electrons, generating oxygen gas
  • Some have only one photosystem, either type I or type II
  • Phototrophic bacteria with only type II use light to make ATP by photophosphorylation
    • Uses membrane-local ATP synthase complex, light energy oxidizes chlorophyll, electron transport chain
    • Terminal electron acceptor is oxidized chlorophyll, cyclic electron flow
    • Cannot fix CO2 because can’t use light energy to make NADH or NADPH
  • Bacteria with only type I can be true photoautotrophs
    • Light oxidizes reaction center chlorophyll, reduces NAD+ to NADH
    • Must be reduced by electrons from chemical electron donor such as hydrogen sulfide
    • Oxidized chlorophyll pulls electrons from H2S down ETC, generating proton gradient for ATP production
    • Use light energy for both ATP and reducing power, both needed for carbon fixation

Oxygenic Photosynthesis and Non-Cyclic Electron Flow

  • Cyanobacteria evolved way to couple both types of photosystems with non-cyclic electron flow
  • Often called Z-scheme
  • Oxidized photosystem II gives electrons to ETC while oxidized photosystem I gives electrons to reduce NADP+ to NADPH
    • Systems linked because oxidized PSI is reduced by ETC
    • Oxidized PSII regains electrons from oxidizing water molecules to generate O2
    • Flow of electrons from water through PSII, down ETC to PSI, finally to NADP+ to make NADPH
  • Ability to use water molecules as electron donors gave evolutionary advantage

Photosynthetic Membranes

  • Convoluted via infolding of plasma membrane, multiplying surface area for light absorption
    • Also present in chloroplasts, evolved from endosymbiotic cyanobacteria
  • Infolded membranes are called thylakoids, lumen corresponds to extracellular or periplasmic space of cyanobacteria
  • Photosynthesis in chloroplasts is essentially same as in cyanobacteria
    • Use non-cyclic electron flow to produce ATP, NADPH, O2

Cyclic Electron Flow in Cyanobacteria and Chloroplasts

  • When demands require more ATP, but not NADPH, cyclic flow from PSI to ETC, back to PSI can boost photophosphorylation
  • Oxidized PSI can give electrons to either NADP+ or to the ETC when needed
