4.01 Mitosis & Meiosis

The Cell Division Cycle

  • 4 stages of division:
    • G1: period right after division, before DNA replication
      • Monitor environment to determine whether to start another round of division
    • S: period of DNA synthesis, cells replicate chromosomes
    • G2: period between DNA replication & start of cell division
      • Check to make sure DNA replication successfully completed, make repairs
    • M: actual period of cell division
      • Prophase, metaphase, anaphase, telophase, cytokinesis

Chromosomes

  • Condense and become visible by light microscopy as eukaryotic clls enter mitosis/meiosis
    • During interphase (G1, S, G2) they are fully or partially decondensed as chromatin
      • Chromatin: DNA wound around histone proteins (nucleosomes)
  • Result of replication: each prophase chromosome consists of pair of chromatids
    • Each chromatid contains linear DNA molecule identical to joined sister

Mitosis

  • Produces two daughter cells genetically identical to each other & parental cell
  • Begins with cell with 2N chromosomes, 4x DNA content because each chromosome has replicated
  • Ends with 2 cells, each with 2N chromosomes and 2x DNA content
  • All eukaryotic cells replicate via mitosis except for eggs & sperm
  • Phases:
    • Prophase - chromosomes condense
    • Metaphase - chromosomes line up at middle of the cell, along plane of division
      • Pushed & puled by microtubules of spindle apparatus
    • Anaphase - sister chromatids separate and migrate towards opposite ends of cell
    • Telophase - chromatids cluster at opposite ends of the cell, begin to decondense
    • Cytokinesis - membrane pinches in to divide two daughter cells

Meiosis

  • Produces haploid gametes from diploid cells
  • Starts with diploid cell that has replicated: 2N chromosomes, 4x DNA content
  • 2 successive divisions, with no additional DNA replication, results in 4 haploid gametes
    • 1N chromosomes, 1x DNA content
  • Sets stage for Mendelian genetics
  • Most genetic action occurs in first division
    • Homologous chromosomes pair up & align end-to-end in prophase I
    • Crossing over between homologous chromosomes in prophase I, before they line up at metaphase plate
    • Homologous chromosomes separate to daughter cells in first division, creating haploid cells
      • Sister chromatids do not separate
    • Separation of each pair occurs independently, so all combinations of maternal & parternal chromosomes are possible
    • First division is when daughter cells become functionally haploid
      • X & Y chromosomes pair in prophase I, separate in first division
      • Daughter cells have either X or Y, but not both
      • Each cell only has one sex chromosomes, like haploid
  • Second meiotic division is where sister chromatids separate
    • Resembles haploid mitosis
