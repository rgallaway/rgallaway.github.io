4.02 Mendelian Genetics

Terminology

  • Hereditary elements are passed on in discrete units (genes) rather than blended together at each generation
  • Gene - hereditary factor that determines specific trait
    • Comprised of specific DNA sequence, located on specific region of specific chromosome
    • Also called genetic locus
  • Allele - particular variant of a gene
  • Genotype - particular collection of alleles in organism’s DNA
    • Homozygous: two of the same alleles for particular gene
    • Heterozygous: two different alleles for particular gene
  • Phenotype - observable traits
    • Some alleles mask others in dominant/recessive pattern
    • Dominant allele produces phenotype whether organism is homozygous or heterozygous at locus
    • Recessive allele only produces phenotype when homozygous at the locus
    • A “carrier” is heterozygous for a gene: phenotypically normal, but carries a copy of the recessive allele

Crosses with a Single Trait and the Principle of Segregation

  • Mendel’s pea plant experiments illustrate above concepts
    • Pea plants have many traits caused by a single gene with simple dominant/recessive pattern
    • Each gamete contains only one allele for each trait
  • Principle of Segregation: idea that each gamete only carries one allele
    • two alleles for a particular trait are segregated into different gametes

Crosses with Two Traits and the Principle of Independent Assortment

  • Alleles for different traits are segregated independently of each other
    • Illustrated by Punnett squares with two or more traits
  • Principle of Independent Assortment: idea that alleles for different traits are segregated independently

Mendel’s Laws and Meiosis

  • Segregation occurs because each gamete only inherits one copy of each chromosome
    • Occurs when homologous chromosomes separate in anaphase I
  • Independent assortment happens because homologous chromosomes are randomly segregated into different gametes
    • Occurs when homologous chromosomes align randomly at metaphase plate during metaphase I
  • Consider cell with set of 2 chromosomes (n = 2): two possible arrangements at equatorial plane
    • Total number of different gametes is 2n, so 4 possible combinations
    • With n = 23 in human cells, over 8 million possible genotype combinations
