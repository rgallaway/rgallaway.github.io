1.07 Earth History

Geology, Earth History, and Biological Evolution

  • Changing geological conditions affect natural selection
  • Fossil record study relies on geology to find clues about evolution

Fossils are found in sedimentary rocks

  • Three types of rocks:
    • Igneous - formed from cooling lava, organismal remnants are destroyed by heat
    • Metamorphic - structure altered by heat & pressure within Earthâ€™s crust, fossils are destroyed
    • Sedimentary - formed by deposition of sand and both inorganic & organic debris, may contain fossils

Dating and Ordering the Geological Record

  • Stratigraphy (study of layers) applies to sedimentary formations
  • Newer layers always on top of older layers, vertical dig reveals timeline
    • Crustal movements and uplifts can cause layers to tilt, fold, or invert

Radiometric Dating

  • Radioisotope decay can estimate absolute ages of rocks
  • Potassium-40 decays to Argon-40, half life = 1.26b yrs, used to date igneous rocks
  • Uranium-238 decays to Lead-206, half life = 4.5b yrs, used to date zircons
  • Carbon-14 decays to nitrogen-14, half life = 5715 yrs, used to date organic matter, measured in relation to C-12
    • Not used to date rocks/fossils millions of years old, like dinosaur bones
    • Instead, date igneous rocks above/below sedimentary rocks containing fossils

Co-Evolution of Life and the Planet

  • Oxygen
    • Early Earth had no oxygen
    • Water-splitting and oxygen-generating photosynthesis by cyanobacteria led to increase in free oxygen, precipitated iron oxide
    • Oxygen led to aerobic metabolism, evolution of eukaryotes & multicellular organisms
    • Oxygen in upper atmosphere formed ozone layer, allowing colonization of land
    • Burial of organic matter in anaerobic sediments allowed oxygen to accumulate in atmosphere
  • Temperature and sea level
    • Large swings in global temperature
      • Factors included changes in solar output, volcanos, meteors, greenhouse gases
