1.06 Speciation

What is a species? Species Concepts

  • Three prominent definitions:
    • Biological species concept: ability of two individuals to successfully produce viable & fertile offspring
    • Morphological species concept: whether two individuals look similar
    • Phylogenetic species concept: how closely related individuals are evolutionarily
  • Use of concepts is based on available data & circumstance
    • Mastodons are extinct, so we can’t use BSC
    • Comparing records somewhat works for MSC, but no complete fossils
    • Fossil & DNA evidence allows phylogenetic grouping into species
  • Ecological species concept: individuals capable of interbreeding may never meet in the wild
    • Species is set of organisms exploiting single niche
    • Key aspects are resources exploited and habitat occupied

Sympatric & Allopatric Speciation

  • Allopatric Speciation
    • Two populations are isolated, lack of gene flow
    • Small changes in each occur over time, some preventing successful reproduction
    • When barrier is removed, the populations can no longer interbreed
  • Sympatric Speciation
    • Two populations in same location can’t interbreed
    • Reproductive isolation reduces gene flow, can be pre- or post-zygotic
      • Pre-zygotic: behavioral differences, different mating time/location, sperm-egg incompatibility
      • Post-zygotic: developmental failures, sterile offspring
