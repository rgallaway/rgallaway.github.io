1.08 Origin of Life on Earth

Origin of Life Studies

  • Scientists can formulate & test hypotheses to account to intermediate steps
    • Formation of organic molecules
    • Formation of polymers of organic molecules that function as enzymes, encode hereditary information, possibly replicate
    • Formation of protocells

How did organic molecules form on a pre-biotic Earth?

  • Miller-Urey Experiment
    • Produced organic molecules, including amino acids
    • Simulated atmosphere with reducing compounds methane, ammonia, hydrogen
    • Sparks simulated lightning, caused chemical reactions that produced organic molecules
    • Pre-biotic Earth not believed to have such a reducing atmosphere, but deep-sea vents have similar environments
  • Organic molecules from meteors
    • Some space dust and meteors contain organic molecules
    • Many more would have fallen 4 billion years ago
  • 3 major sources of organic molecules
    • Atmospheric synthesis (Miller-Urey)
    • deep-sea hydrothermal synthesis
    • in-fall of organic molecules from space
    • At high enough concentrations, they can link to form polymers

Enzymatic activity and hereditary information in one polymer: the RNA World hypothesis

  • Thomas Cech discovered that some RNA can catalyze site-specific cleavage
    • Called “ribozymes”
  • Populations of ribozymes would undergo evolution similar to biological natural selection
  • Modern ribosomes are giant ribozymes

Protocells: self-replicating and metabolic enzymes in a bag

  • Cells’ lipid membranes allow them to maintain high concentrations of nucleotides for self-replicating RNA
  • Lipids are hydrophobic, self-assemble in water forming micelles or lipid bilayer vesicles
    • Vesicles enclosing self-replicating RNA & enzymes, take in reactants, export products, grow by accretion, divide by fission, are proto-cells
