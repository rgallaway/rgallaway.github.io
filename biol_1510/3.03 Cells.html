3.03 Cells

Prokaryotes

  • First cells resembled bacteria or archaea: simple structures, no nuclei or internal organelles
  • Last Universal Common Ancestor
    • Simple structure, phospholipid bilayer membrane, no nuclei or organelles
  • Bacteria and archaea are prokaryotes, all have:
    • single circular chromosome (some species have 2)
    • nucleoid region containing chromosome, no membrane separating from cytoplasm
    • small circular DNA plasmids dispersed in cytoplasm
    • cell walls that differ between Bacteria and Archaea
    • rudimentary cytoskeleton, can have flagella

Evolution of Eukaryotes

  • About 2.1-2.4 million years ago, soon after Great Oxygenation Event
  • Membranes have sterols, which require oxygen to synthesize
  • Genes for nuclear proteins are similar to Archaea, but metabolism & lipid synthesis genes resemble Bacteria
    • Hypothesis that eukaryotes evolved from endosymbiosis between Archaeon and Bacterium
  • Required many innovations
    • Endocytosis: prokaryotes lack endo- & phagocytosis, essential for acquiring endosymbionts
      • can lead to development of internal endomembrane system
      • Can account for formation of nuclear envelope, endomembrane, mitochondria & chloroplasts

Eukaryotic Cell Structure

  • Major cell systems
  • Endomembrane System
    • Includes nuclear envelope, endoplasmic reticulum, Golgi complex, lysosomes, transport vesicles, secretory vesicles, endosomes, plasma membrane
    • Membrane proteins & secretion proteins are made in the rough ER by ribosomes, in unfinished form
      • They move to smooth ER, are partially glycosylated, membrane lipids are made in smooth ER
      • Transport vesicles form around proteins, travel to the Golgi, add contents to Golgi vesicles
      • Sorted and processed, additional glycosylation, lysosomal proteins pinch off and become lysosomes
        • Secretory proteins move into secretory vesicles, fuse with plasma membrane
    • Does not include mitochondria or chloroplasts, proteins made by free cytoplasmic ribosomes
  • Cytoskeleton
    • Actin microfilaments, intermediate filaments, microtubules
    • Microfilaments form networks near plasma membrane to stabilize or change cell shape
    • Microtubules serve as tracks for transport vesicles and secretory vesicles, chromosomes during division
  • Extracellular Matrix
    • Outside, overlaying plasma membrane
    • Cell wall in plants & yeast, collagen & protein/polysaccharide polymer in animal cells
  • Nucleus
    • Contains chromosomes, DNA replication & transcription, RNA processing
    • Enzymes for above, proteins to bind DNA, all proteins in nucleus are made by cytoplasmic ribosomes
    • Ribosomal & messenger RNA are made in nucleus, exit through pore complexes
